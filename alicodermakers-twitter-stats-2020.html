27 December 2020


One of my 2020 goals was to grow on Twitter. I have been trying to get it going since the year started.


2020 in Numbers

  • 721,100 impressions.
  • 249 new followers.
  • 2010 tweets in 2020.
  • 167.5 Average tweets/month.
  • 331 tweets with 95,700 impressions in August.
  • 92 tweets with 26,900 impressions in May.

When I look back at the year, I think I got to learn something new every month. I wouldn't say I didn't waste time, but I could have spend less time while getting the same results. In the next sentence, I talk about my plan to waste less time on twitter.


My goal for 2021 is to reach 1,500,000 people.


To help me with this goal, I am working on a chrome extension to sends tweets without opening Twitter. This extension will stop me from getting distracted on the Twitter feed.


The Issue

I want to be active on Twitter without wasting too much time. Once I am on the Twitter feed, I end up spending way too much time scrolling the infinite page. I want to consume Twitter within limits. But I want to contribute on Twitter limitless.

you see the issue here? How can I contribute on Twitter, without opening Twitter app or website?

The tool is centred around people who tweet their thoughts as and when they come.


My take on Growing on Twitter

Besides having a personality, I have realised there are two things you need to grow on Twitter.

  1. Tweet consistently, and
  2. engage on other's tweet.

The idea is to reply to other people's tweets and present yourself as an engaging user. Imagine you are at a party and need to make friends. Take part in other people's conversation and establish your validity.

When a typical Twitter user sees a quality reply, they visit your profile. and look at your original tweets and retweets.


Quirks I have noticed on Twitter

From personal behaviour. I have noticed that some people would like a tweet simply because it has lots of likes.

Basically, if some tweet is going viral, people want to a part of it.
